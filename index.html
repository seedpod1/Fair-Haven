üö® CRITICAL: Medical Device Cyber-Physical Risk - NO COVERAGE

The "No Man's Land" Between Cyber and Medical Malpractice Insurance

Your current insurance program leaves you completely unprotected for one of healthcare's most dangerous emerging risks:

‚ö†Ô∏è The Scenario:

Malware compromises a connected medical device (patient monitor, infusion pump, vital signs equipment), making it inoperable or causing it to fail to alert staff to a critical patient condition. A patient dies or suffers serious injury as a result.

‚úó What Your Cyber Policy EXCLUDES:

  • Section IV A(ii): "We will not cover loss or first party loss in connection with any claim or cyber incident for bodily injury"
  • Definition includes: "any actual or alleged bodily injury, sickness, disease, death, emotional distress or mental anguish"
  • Limited exception does NOT apply: Exception only covers "mental anguish or humiliation" from "privacy wrongful act" - NOT death from security breach

URGENT ACTION REQUIRED: This is a "bet the organization" exposure. A single incident could bankrupt Fair Haven if uninsured. You must either (1) verify medical malpractice covers technology-caused injuries, (2) obtain limited bodily injury endorsement on your cyber policy, or (3) implement device isolation procedures to eliminate the risk.

Other Critical Insurance Gaps Identified

  • Tracking Pixel Exclusion: Potential $800K-$3.3M uninsured exposure from OCR enforcement
  • Fraud Coverage Gap: Current $250K limits vs. $650K average healthcare BEC loss
  • Aggregate Limit: $5M coverage vs. $10.93M average healthcare breach cost

‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL: Business Interruption Coverage Ambiguity

Your Policy DOES Include Systems Failure Coverage, BUT...

Contradictory language creates dangerous uncertainty about your most likely business interruption scenario:

‚úì What's Clearly Covered:

  • "System Failure" = unintentional/unplanned network outage preventing business operations
  • 8-hour waiting period, then $5M limit with $10K retention
  • Dependent business system failures (third-party vendors)

‚úó The Contradiction:

  • Network definition (Page 19): INCLUDES "IT provider" and "cloud computing" - suggests coverage
  • BUT Critical Infrastructure Exclusion (Page 32): EXCLUDES "infrastructure operated by third party"
  • Result: Unclear if Epic/Cerner/Allscripts EHR outage would be covered

üö® Why This Matters for Fair Haven:

  • Cloud-based EHR outage is your #1 most likely BI trigger
  • 4-day outage = $656,000 in lost revenue and continuing expenses
  • Epic (2018), Cerner (2020), Allscripts (2018) all had multi-day outages affecting healthcare providers
  • Without written clarification, you may pay $21,770/year for coverage that doesn't respond when needed

ACTION REQUIRED: Obtain written endorsement from CNA explicitly stating that contracted cloud service provider outages ARE covered and Critical Infrastructure exclusion does NOT apply to your EHR vendor.
